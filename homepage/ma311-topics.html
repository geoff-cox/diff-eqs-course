    Subsection 1.4 ODP Topics

    In this course we use proficiency-based grading (PBG), sometimes called mastery-based grading. Instead of focusing on points, PBG emphasizes demonstrating understanding of key concepts. You will have multiple opportunities to show proficiency, and you are not penalized for mistakes made on early attempts. The goal is to ensure you eventually master the essential skills of the course.
    🔗

    How ODPs Work.

    ODPs are short assessments that let you show proficiency with specific topics. Each ODP is scored as:
    • P: Proficient
      🔗
    • N: Not yet proficient
      🔗
    🔗
    ODPs may be in-class or out-of-class. Out-of-class ODPs allow you to use some notes, a formula sheet, and basic calculator. In-class ODPs are closed book, with only the formula sheet and basic calculator allowed. Core topics appear on both types of ODPs; secondary topics appear only on in-class ODPs.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsubsection Core Topics

    There are 7 core topics that represent the most essential concepts of the course. To demonstrate proficiency in a core topic, you must pass it twice, with at least one pass occurring on an in-class ODP. Out-of-class ODPs usually offer each core topic three times, so you have multiple chances to succeed. If you do not pass a topic outside class, you can still demonstrate proficiency through in-class attempts.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsubsection Secondary Topics

    In addition to the core topics, there are 13 secondary topics. These cover supporting skills and applications. You only need to pass each secondary topic once, and they are only assessed on in-class ODPs. While you must pass all core topics to earn a C or higher, proficiency in secondary topics can help raise your grade to a B or an A.
    🔗
    Note: This secondary topics list is tentative and subject to change.
